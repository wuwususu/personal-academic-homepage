About

Research Profile

My previous research has focused on respiratory and infectious disease questions, including differential diagnosis, pulmonary disease, invasive fungal disease, and immune-related clinical topics. I have contributed to research supported by China's National Science and Technology Major Project for 2030 and participated in clinical guideline development, which shaped my interest in translating evidence into practical standards for diagnosis and care. I work across clinical epidemiology and evidence-based medicine, using systematic reviews, meta-analyses, Mendelian randomization, and machine-learning approaches to connect clinical observations with reproducible evidence. Building on this respiratory research background, I am preparing for PhD study in ophthalmology, where I hope to apply rigorous clinical research methods and data-driven tools to eye disease, visual health, and translational questions in ophthalmic care.

  • Research experience connected to China's National Science and Technology Major Project for 2030
  • Participation in clinical guideline development and evidence translation
  • Respiratory medicine and infectious disease research experience
  • Evidence synthesis through systematic review and meta-analysis
  • Genetic epidemiology and Mendelian randomization studies
  • Machine-learning-assisted clinical diagnosis and decision support
  • Future PhD direction in ophthalmology and visual health
